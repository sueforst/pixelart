Universe

The universe (Latin: universus) is all of space and time[a] and their contents, [10] including planets, stars, galaxies, and all other forms of matter and energy.
While the spatial size of the entire universe is unknown,[3] it is possible to measure the size of the observable universe,
which is currently estimated to be 93 billion light-years in diameter.
In various multiverse hypotheses, a universe is one of many causally disconnected[11] constituent parts of a larger multiverse,
which itself comprises all of space and time and its contents.[12]
